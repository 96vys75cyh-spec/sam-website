What SAM does

A decision architecture, not a sensor

Sensors and monitoring devices are already very good at observing and reporting what is happening. SAM sits above them, using the information they already provide to support one justified answer: is it still safe to wait, or has the moment for action arrived?

SAM is not

  • A new sensor or piece of hardware
  • A monitoring or alarm system
  • A replacement for existing telecare or clinical infrastructure
  • A generative AI system

SAM is

  • An explainable decision architecture that sits above the systems already in place
  • Designed to help turn existing signals into a justified decision
  • Designed to help reduce unnecessary escalation while supporting timely action when it matters most
  • Deterministic, explainable and auditable by design, rather than dependent on opaque machine-learning models

Founder

Founded by Annabett Wain

AW

Annabett Wain founded Smart Access and Monitoring Ltd to pursue a different question in care technology: not just what has changed, but whether it is still safe to wait. SAM is being developed as a research-led, explainable approach to that question, working alongside independent academic partners.
